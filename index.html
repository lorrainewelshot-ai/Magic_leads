Your physical & sensory environment
Quick audit of your workspace

Sensory factors significantly affect focus and task initiation. Answering yes means this is currently in place for you.

Are you regularly interrupted by noise — chatter, equipment, office traffic?Background noise raises your sensory load and makes initiating or sustaining tasks harder
Is there a lot of visual clutter or movement in your workspace?Peripheral movement and desk clutter pull attention away from the task at hand
Does your workspace have access to natural light or a daylight lamp?Lighting quality affects alertness and sustained attention throughout the day
Can you access a quiet space when you need to reduce sensory input?Having a retreat option reduces the compounding effect of sensory overload on focus
3
Tools, processes & support
What's already working — and what's missing

These are practical scaffolds that reduce cognitive load. Tick yes if you have them; no if they're a gap.

Tools & process
Social & accountability
Do you have visible reminders — wall calendar, sticky notes, phone alarms — to track deadlines?External memory aids reduce the burden on working memory
Are your task instructions provided in writing rather than verbally only?Written instructions remove reliance on working memory to hold multi-step directions
Do you use structured templates for recurring tasks like emails or reports?Templates eliminate the decision fatigue of starting from a blank page
Have you scheduled protected focus time or "no distraction" blocks in your day?Planned white space reduces the mental cost of unexpected interruptions
Are your physical and digital files colour-coded or clearly labelled?Visual organisation systems reduce the search time that derails momentum
Do you have someone who checks in on your progress — a manager, mentor, or trusted colleague?Body doubling and external accountability significantly improve task initiation and follow-through
Do you receive positive acknowledgement for completed tasks, not just correction when things go wrong?Positive feedback loops support motivation and self-regulation
Have you communicated your preferred ways of receiving information (e.g. written vs verbal)?Matching communication to your processing style reduces cognitive effort significantly
